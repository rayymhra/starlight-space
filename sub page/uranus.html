Summary

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and the third-largest in the Solar System. It is an ice giant, composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and icy materials like water, methane, and ammonia. Unlike any other planet, Uranus rotates on its side, making its seasons extreme. It has a faint ring system and dozens of moons, most of which are named after characters from Shakespearean plays.
