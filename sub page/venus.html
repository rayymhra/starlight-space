Summary

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called Earth's 'sister planet' due to its similar size and composition. However, Venus has a harsh, inhospitable environment with a thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet in the Solar System. It has a rocky surface covered with mountains, volcanoes, and vast plains. Venus is known for its slow rotation and extreme greenhouse effect, which keeps its surface temperature high enough to melt lead.
