Summary

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun, making it the most distant known planet in the Solar System. It is an ice giant, composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and icy materials like water, ammonia, and methane. Neptune is known for its deep blue color, strong winds, and dynamic storms, including the famous Great Dark Spot. It has a faint ring system and 14 known moons, the largest of which is Triton.
